More precisely, I use electronic structure theory (density functional theory and N-body GW corrections) applied to carbon-based and low-dimensional materials. During my PhD I have focused on the study of electron-phonon interaction, with emphasis on conventional superconductivity and charge density waves. Lately, I have focused on optical properties (excitons) of polymers and molecules and their interaction with phonons (exciton-phonon interaction). I also work closely with experimental groups, for which I provide theoretical support for transport measurements in two-dimensional systems and vibrational properties of materials (such as Raman spectroscopy).
